Summary

Although there are many applications in the current store that deal with hiking (or other outdoors activities that benefit from a map and modern device GPS functionality), those applications focus primarily on just sports and not tourism.

On the other hand, applications that do focus on tourism, such as Tripadvisor, usually only target urban, rather than nature, tourism. Tripadvisor, for example, can suggest you visit a certain park, but once you're there, it won't suggest what you should do there, what places are worth visiting inside, where can you see nice views in that park.

An application that combines both, moderated content from commercial tourism. As well as user content (such as worthwhile tails, pictures and reviews), as well as a powerful parameterized search & recommendation engine, will be able to attract younger tourists that like outdoor activities as well as the occasional urban attraction.

Monetization

For our application to be sustainable, a monetization model is required to support maintenance, future development and the infrastructure(backend servers). I suggest trying to monetize from businesses that want to appear(or be somehow highlighted) on the map of attractions in the application. In my opinion this model can be the best compromise between good user experience and sustainability.

Requirements

  1. Authentication via Google.
  2. User interface for managing content such as: opening hours, description, address, phone number, etc
  3. Map interface (from Google or OpenStreetView) that shows the managed content.
  4. Ability to record and provide information such as suitability for trails, and upload them to the cloud.
  5. Display all the recorded trails on a map and allow users to navigate by them.
  6. Add user content such as reviews, pictures, ratings and points of interest for normal users.
  7. Build a wish list of activities.
  8. Search trails by parameters(same way AllTrails does).
  9. Search other attractions(that are managed).
  10. History of all the places you've looked at and content you've uploaded.
