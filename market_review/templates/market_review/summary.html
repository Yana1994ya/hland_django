Summary

Although there are many applications in the current store that deal with hiking (or other outdoors activities that benefit from a map and modern device GPS function), those applications focus primarily on just sports and not tourism.

On the other hand, applications that do focus on tourism, such as trip advisor, usually only target urban, rather than nature, tourism. Tripadvisor, for example, can suggest you visit a certain park, but once you're there, it won't suggest what you should do there, what places are worth visiting inside, where can you see nice views in that park.

An application that combines both, moderated content from commercial tourism (where business owners/employees can edit data regarding their business). As well as user content (such as worthwhile tails, pictures and reviews) as well as a powerful parameterised search & recommendation engine, will be able to attract younger tourists that like outdoor activities as well as the occasional urban attraction.

Monetization

For our application to be sustainable, a monetization model is required to support maintenance, future development and the infrastructure(backend servers). I suggest trying to monetize businesses that want to appear(or be somehow highlighted) on the map of attractions in the application. In my opinion this model can be the best compromise between good user experience and sustainability.
